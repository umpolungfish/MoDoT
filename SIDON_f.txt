# A Probabilistic Alteration Proof of (s(X)\gg X^{1/3})

## Theorem

There exists an absolute constant (c>0) such that for all sufficiently large (X),

$$
s(X)\ge cX^{1/3}.
$$

## Proof

Let

$$
B\subset [1,X]
$$

be formed by including each integer independently with probability

$$
p=\lambda X^{-2/3},
$$

where (\lambda>0) is a sufficiently small constant.

Then

$$
\mathbb E|B|
============

# Xp

\lambda X^{1/3}.
$$

We now remove elements from (B) in order to eliminate all nontrivial additive relations.

Let (Z) denote the number of nontrivial solutions

$$
a+b=c+d
$$

with

$$
a,b,c,d\in B,
$$

where

$$
{a,b}\neq{c,d}.
$$

We split the count into two cases.

### Four distinct elements

Suppose (a,b,c,d) are distinct. The number of such solutions in ([1,X]) is

$$
O(X^3),
$$

because one may choose three of the variables freely and the fourth is determined.

Each survives in (B) with probability (p^4). Hence the expected number of these relations is

$$
O(X^3p^4).
$$

Substituting (p=\lambda X^{-2/3}),

$$
X^3p^4
======

# X^3\lambda^4X^{-8/3}

\lambda^4X^{1/3}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\mathbb E Z_4
\ll
\lambda^4X^{1/3}.
$$

### Three distinct elements

The remaining possibility is a relation of the form

$$
2a=c+d
$$

with

$$
a,c,d
$$

distinct.

There are only

$$
O(X^2)
$$

such triples, and each occurs with probability (p^3). Therefore

$$
\mathbb E Z_3
\ll
X^2p^3.
$$

Since

$$
X^2p^3
======

# X^2\lambda^3X^{-2}

\lambda^3,
$$

we have

$$
\mathbb E Z_3=O(1).
$$

Combining the two cases,

$$
\mathbb E Z
\ll
\lambda^4X^{1/3}+O(1).
$$

## Alteration

For every surviving nontrivial additive relation, delete one element involved in that relation. After all deletions, the remaining set (B') is Sidon.

If (Z) relations survive, at most (Z) elements are deleted, so

$$
|B'|\ge |B|-Z.
$$

Taking expectations,

$$
\mathbb E|B'|
\ge
\mathbb E|B|-\mathbb E Z .
$$

Therefore,

$$
\mathbb E|B'|
\ge
\lambda X^{1/3}
---------------

## C\lambda^4X^{1/3}

O(1).
$$

Choose (\lambda>0) so small that

$$
C\lambda^4\le \frac{\lambda}{2}.
$$

Then for sufficiently large (X),

$$
\mathbb E|B'|
\ge
\frac{\lambda}{3}X^{1/3}.
$$

Since (B') is a random variable taking only nonnegative integer values, there exists at least one realization satisfying

$$
|B'|
\ge
\frac{\lambda}{3}X^{1/3}.
$$

Thus

$$
s(X)\gg X^{1/3}.
$$

(\square)

---

## Remark on the limitation of the method

The exponent (1/3) is forced by the alteration balance.

For a random set of density (p),

$$
\mathbb E|B|\asymp Xp,
$$

while the expected number of additive quadruples is

$$
\asymp X^3p^4.
$$

To make deletion affordable one requires

$$
X^3p^4\lesssim Xp,
$$

which gives

$$
p^3\lesssim X^{-2},
$$

hence

$$
p\lesssim X^{-2/3}.
$$

The resulting size is therefore

$$
Xp\lesssim X^{1/3}.
$$

So this alteration argument cannot reach the optimal Sidon scale (X^{1/2}). The stronger lower bound requires additional structure, such as the algebraic constructions arising from finite geometry or the Erdős–Turán construction.
