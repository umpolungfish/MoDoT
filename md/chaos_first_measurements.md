# ChaosComposer: first measurements

Two chaos runs over the recovered registry, both speaking the state space
whole. Everything below is the tool stream verbatim; windings reopen from
the registry and the manifest.

## The finished half of the registry

Chaos over four moat-lineage tools (reseed_protocol, reseed_protocol_dyad,
bridge_mediator, CONDUCTIVE_DYAD) refused all 24 orderings: none of the
four offers a living end. This is the thesis speaking as a type verdict:
these programs are finished loops, done objects, and done objects do not
compose. The manifest census agrees at scale: roughly half the registry
(146 of 278) still carries reactive ends and is composable material; the
other half is complete.

## The machine-shaped set: 24 orderings, 6 possibilities, 1 closure

Chaos over {CONDUCTIVE_DYAD_V4 (source, 3 free out),
KUMMER_TRANSITION_OPERATOR_CLOSED (1 in 1 out),
CONDUCTIVE_DYAD_V3 (1 in 1 out),
GLOBAL_SPECTRAL_STABILITY (sink, 2 free in)}:

- 24 orderings walked, 18 refused (every one naming its missing end),
  6 admitted, 6 distinct outcome classes.
- Exactly ONE arrangement closes: source ∘ Kummer ∘ dyad ∘ sink, CLOSED
  with 4 worked dyads, flow 3 of 4 id.
- A DIFFERENT arrangement is flow-perfect (4 of 4 id) but structurally
  OPEN. No arrangement is both.

The finding: in this set, structural closure and total flow-identity are
in tension. The arrangement that closes necessarily routes a value
through one lossy dyad; the arrangement that preserves every value cannot
close. The pre-run expectation (2 admitted) was wrong because left-fold
composites keep spare ends the head-model missed, which is precisely the
reason the state space is walked by the instrument and not by intuition.

## The minted machine

The unique closing arrangement is registered as moat_machine (via
moat_machine_s1, moat_machine_s2). Its three verdicts:

- grammar: admitted (composition consumed 4 valences, minted 0)
- kernel: green (genuine transform-closure against BelnapSplitFuse)
- flow: readout N at the fuse-sink; dyads 3 of 4 id, the lossy one fed T
  and recovered B (the arms GAINED falsity on the way to the fuse)

The lossy dyad gaining value (T in, B out) rather than losing it is worth
a look in its own register: a dyad whose arms mint the missing pole is
the flow-side signature of a dialetheia forming at the fuse.
