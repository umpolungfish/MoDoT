Add the click-maths spec and settle the math catalytic register

Two things landed. First, the register: I ported red-hot_rebis's enzyme
catalytic-site engine to abstract math by imscribing a slate of mathematical
operations and reading where they land. The result is that math does NOT use
all 12 primitives — three are pinned, and they're exactly the physical axes,
because math abstracts physics out: Fidelity pinned high (everything exact),
Criticality pinned low (nothing is a phase transition), Kinetics suppressed
(no energy barriers, broken only by winding_number, which is why it's the lone
O_2 object). The pins sit on one member each of three conjugate pairs, so
catalysis runs on the three live pairs D<->W, T<->H, R<->S. Smaller, cleaner
design space than an enzyme active site.

The method lesson worth keeping: I tried to rescue the collapsed Kinetics and
Criticality by re-phrasing the residues as verbs instead of nouns
("differentiating" not "the derivative operator"). It failed — the Grammar
reads structure, not wording. Differentiation is genuinely kinetically frozen
(smooth, reversible), a fixed point is genuinely subcritical (an attractor, not
a critical point). The failure is the finding.

Second, CLICK_MATHS_SPEC.md — the first concrete build off the register. Click
chemistry is spring-loaded, orthogonal, always-closing fusion, which is just
reliable FFUSE of two fragments complementary on a live pair. It's the smallest
delta from the FSPLIT/FFUSE the prover already has, and it's a speedup: if two
proven leaves are click-complementary you already know they fuse, so you skip
the assembly search. The complementarity/orthogonality checks and the closure
certificate are mechanizable now; the library of named clicks is designed until
each archetype is imscribed and gated.
