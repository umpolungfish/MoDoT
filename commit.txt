Collapse degenerate reasoning loops before they become the answer

Live telemetry from the last jam showed the PROD round derail: instead of
emitting a TOOL: line the model returned the same two lines alternating
forty times ("Wait: output the TOOL lines / Okay: write the final answer").
That text carries no TOOL: line, so the ACT loop breaks immediately and the
loop itself becomes the answer — and if it lands mid-round it bloats
agent_msgs on top of that. The membrane still held (fused=N, nothing
ungrounded got a truth-value), but a wall of repetition reached the operator
as the visible reply.

Fixed at the one choke point every provider passes through: infer() now runs
collapse_degenerate() on the returned text. If the output is dominated by a
tiny set of lines — four or fewer distinct, or distinct lines under a quarter
of the total across 12+ lines — it is a loop, not prose, and collapses to its
distinct lines in order with a marker. The test is on the distinct-to-total
ratio, not adjacency, because the derailment alternates A/B/A/B and a
consecutive-dup pass would sail right past it. Short or varied answers are
returned untouched, so an honest reply never trips the guard.

Two unit tests lock it: the exact alternating loop reduces to its two lines,
and a varied verdict passes through byte-for-byte. Builds clean, 23 tests
pass.
