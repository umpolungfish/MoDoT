Land the 49 primitive-type natures in the vessel

I uncovered the primitive types, which revealed IMASM, which I used to
elaborate the types back into their full natures. Those elaborations are
the auto-designed ob3ects: each one carries a completed 12-family
Imscription tuple whose values are themselves drawn from the same 49
names, so the alphabet is its own value set and the natures close into
an ouroboric algebra. This brings them into MoDoT.

ob3ects/primitives/ holds the 49 nature ob3ects (the type_called set;
the prototype egg is left out). modot/natures.py loads each into a
Nature: its canonical 12-family tuple, its phase_1 opcode word (which is
already exactly the MoDoT Token algebra), its Belnap void/true/false/both
readings, its Frobenius verdict and entropy ledger. FAMILY_TO_VESSEL_KEY
bridges the twelve scaffold families onto the vessel's PRIMITIVE_KEYS, so
Nature.vessel_tuple() hands the vessel a grounded imscription for any of
the 49 types by table lookup instead of an LLM round trip.

The registry verifies the algebra is closed on load: every value in
every tuple is one of the 49 known types, no open references. Exposed at
package level as nature(), nature_registry(), Nature, NatureRegistry.
Self-test passes: all 49 load, closed, egg maps cleanly onto the twelve
vessel keys. This is the first plank: the natures are now operators the
vessel and composer can act with, not just glyphs.
