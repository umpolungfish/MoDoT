ROTAT: the first op-opcode

An op-opcode is a third kind of thing, of a different order than the twelve
node-opcodes and the composition verbs: a node-opcode is a symbol inside a word,
a verb turns a word into a graph, an op-opcode maps a whole word to another word.
ROTAT is the first of them, the cyclic shift of a ring, the ring automorphism,
the Weyl-Heisenberg shift X on Z/dZ.

ask_native gains `imasm rotat [k] <word>`: it rotates the word by k and reports
that every spectral invariant is preserved. That invariance is not the operator
being inert, it is the signal that ROTAT is a symmetry of the ring rather than a
transformation of it. On one ring nothing measurable moves; between two rings
being bound it sets their relative phase, the degree of freedom that seats a
junction two same-handed rings cannot close alone. IMASM_QUICKREF gains an
OP-OPCODES section stating the class.

The op was not in the alphabet. The agent working the M136279841 ring reached
for it because binding two isotactic rings needed a degree of freedom no
node-opcode carries, and it named the exact automorphism the whole construction
turns on. This commits the operator it saw.
