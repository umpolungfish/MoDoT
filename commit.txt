Keep the spine and tool runner honest under two failure modes

Two fixes, both surfaced by real jam runs.

1. A material non-closure no longer denies a proven theorem.
   The agent would prove a real theorem (h(k) << k^2, Iwaniec), forge the
   catalog entries naming its parts, find them co-typed (no click), and
   the fuse turned that into B: "True, but does not close / does not
   exist". But forging named entities tests their tuple complementarity,
   a fact about the entries, not the theorem's truth; a non-click is not
   disproof and a closure is not a proof. TOOLS_PROMPT now says so in one
   sentence (and points at prove: for a theorem's real closure), and
   complete() gains a lane guard: on a proof-shaped answer a material
   non-closure abstains (N) instead of voting against the proof. Outside
   jam the proof stays T with no false conflict; in a jam it is N
   (honestly ungrounded, since the tools never tested the theorem), never
   the B self-contradiction. A genuine structural jam keeps its real F.

2. A spawn failure is no longer reported as bad args.
   Structural verbs re-shell to this binary via current_exe(). When the
   binary is rebuilt while a session runs, the old inode is unlinked and
   current_exe() reads back "<path> (deleted)", so every structural call
   failed to spawn and returned None -- which the new arity feedback then
   mislabeled "wrong or too few args" even though the args were correct.
   resolve_self_exe() strips the " (deleted)" suffix and relinks to the
   rebuilt binary at the real path; if it still cannot find or spawn the
   binary, the runner returns a clear "restart ./ask" message rather than
   a None that masquerades as an argument error.

Tests: answer_is_proof recognition; complete() across proof-outside-jam
(T, no conflict), proof-in-jam (N, not B), and a real non-closure (B);
and the deleted-suffix strip.
