The promotion path crosses faces, and says which way it walked.

Three things the path verb got wrong. It read both endpoints in ONE face,
so a classic word handed to a pair whose other member carried tri glyphs
was silently truncated to the glyphs that face parses: the report answered
about a word nobody asked for. It called any verdict change a promotion,
so a walk descending T to N was announced as a climb. And it filtered
argument tokens on "contains =", eating any word carrying CLINK.

Each endpoint now names its own face. When the faces differ the walk
CROSSES: the two grammars share ten glyphs and differ only in the dyad,
binary against ternary, so the search runs the union alphabet with every
waypoint judged in the face its own dyad selects, and a word carrying both
dyads belongs to neither grammar and is refused as a waypoint. The step
where the grammar changes is marked in the report. The crossing found in
practice does not substitute the dyad in place; it dissolves the binary
pair first and walks a face-neutral valley before the ternary pair forms,
which is what the both-dyads refusal forces.

Verdict direction is read off the endpoints under F < N < B < T: climb,
descent, or a wander that returns to its own class.

Also in the loop: each word keeps its residual HISTORY rather than only
its latest, and the run reports first-visit against last-visit residual
over words seen more than once. That measures the model against itself and
is immune to the walk drifting onto easier ground, which the per-run mean
is not.
