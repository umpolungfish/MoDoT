Make the ring materials operable: spectral invariants + forge/compare/dope

The materials my ring verbs produce (conductance CONDUCTIVE/FRUSTRATED/INSULATING,
modulus, stability) were missing the one invariant that actually discriminates
structure — the spectral radius. I was computing it by hand in Python. Now the
engine computes it.

Characterize. The material sheet now carries spectral invariants: I build the
ring's adjacency graph from the real bonds (clean bond = 1, a cross-link
junction = k, its number of reaction centers), take its eigenvalues with a
hand-rolled Jacobi solver (no linalg dependency), and print the spectrum, the
spectral radius ρ, and the spectral gap. ρ = 2 exactly witnesses a pure
unbranched cycle; ρ > 2 witnesses branching — the same fact the topology line
asserts, now as a computed number. Verified exact: the pure 4-ring gives
[+2, 0, 0, -2], and the branched 3-ring (one 2-center cross-link) gives
[+2.732, -0.732, -2], matching lambda^3 - 6 lambda - 4 = 0 by hand.

Operate. Three material-algebra verbs, deterministic (no LLM), as CLI flags and
agent tools:
  forge M1 M2...        one-shot: arrange the set into its best ring and print the
                        full sheet incl. spectral invariants (= --arrange --props)
  compare A B vs X Y    forge two materials and diff them (delta-rho, conductance shift)
  dope A B with C       forge the base, re-forge with the dopant, report the shift

Also told the operator (TOOLS_PROMPT) never to assert rho or conductance without
forging — the numbers come only from the verb, not from narration.
