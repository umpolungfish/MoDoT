Characterize the closed ring as a material -- conductance grounds the "computer" claim

I fed a cyclic polymer (general_recursive_function · grothendieck_topos ·
skolem_normal_form) back through the agent and asked it to "apply it as a material"
and hand me a computer design. The STRUCTURAL layer was grounded -- the ring really
does cyclize (close confirms it on R↔S). But the APPLICATION layer was pure prose:
the model spun an "LTC-1 Topological Processor" with a superconducting sheaf-array,
chi=0 non-dissipative memory, topologically protected gates, and "a current of
effective calculability flowing through the cyclic logic-gates." None of it computed.

The one checkable claim buried in there is that the ring CONDUCTS. So I made that
decidable. --polymerize ... --props (agent verb: material), in ask_native/src/click.rs
(print_ring_material). When the polymer is closed, it characterizes the macrocycle as
a material by reusing the Omega-transfer primitive:
- try to circulate a winding quantum Omega around the ring; one consistent direction
  the whole loop = CONDUCTIVE (a persistent ring current, the carrier integral closes)
- every junction passes a carrier but no single direction closes = FRUSTRATED
- a junction blocks a carrier both ways = INSULATING
- plus the weakest clean ring bond (a ring is only as stable as its weakest link)

The verdict refuted the narrative exactly. The grf/grothendieck/skolem ring is
INSULATING: every unit has Omega = awe (fully oxidized, zero winding quantum), so no
carrier can pass any junction. The ring exists but it is STATIC -- not the dynamic
"topological computer" the prose asserted. Contrast: the moon/logographic macrocycle
carries mid-range Omega and comes back CONDUCTIVE, a persistent ring current. So the
sheet genuinely separates conductor from insulator, from structure alone.

Verified via the agent too: asked whether the ring "works as a computer", the model
now grounds on the material readout and answers "functions as a mathematical
insulator ... Omega-saturated ... a static architectural loop that lacks the dynamic
carrier flow" -- the direct correction of the LTC-1 fantasy.
