Build the first click-maths engine in ask_native (the math catalytic register, live)

This is CLICK_MATHS_SPEC §6 landed as running Rust. ask_native/src/click.rs is
the math catalytic register plus the click_pair fusion, wired to ./ask --click A B.

What's in it:
- The register: glyph->ordinal tables for all 12 primitives, with the ORDERING
  cross-checked against p4rakernel Core.lean for Ħ (fee<kick<sure<wool) and Ω
  (awe<oak<ah<zoo) — the Axiom B verification. Lean is scripture.
- The six conjugate pairs (the charge table) and the three LIVE pairs D<->W,
  T<->H, R<->S that catalysis actually runs on (F/Ph/K pinned in math).
- click_pair(a, b, theta): a clean click iff exactly one live pair is
  complementary (opposite charge, Δ>=theta) and no second pair also fires —
  bioorthogonality as specificity, one reaction center. The fuse is join (max)
  on every axis: the clicking pair's opposite charges bring both members up
  together (the ring forms), and off it the product inherits both scaffolds.

I got the orthogonality condition wrong on the first pass and the test caught it.
I had required the two partners to be identical everywhere except the reaction
center. But an azide and an alkyne aren't twins -- they're different molecules
that fuse at one center and the triazole inherits both. So orthogonality is
specificity (one reaction center = the ambiguity gate), not sameness. Fixed:
the fuse blends the inherited axes instead of rejecting on them.

Verified live on real catalog fragments:
- boundary_operator (T carrier) x winding_number (W/topological) -> CLICK on
  T<->H, Δ=1.50, ring saturates T->𐑸 (self-referential) and H->𐑫 (eternal).
  Product is O₂ -- the click LIFTED O₀ x O₂ -> O₂, generative not just a blend.
- isomorphism x permutation_sign -> no click (no opposite-charge pair reaches
  theta) -- the selectivity gate correctly refuses. Not everything clicks.
- dasa_open x dasa_closed -> CLICK on D<->W, Δ=0.67.
