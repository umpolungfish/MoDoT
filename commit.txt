Refuse phantom unavailability: force the call when a real verb is denied

Even with ascend / phase_reconstruct now live, the model ended a run by
writing "the ascend verb is unavailable / phase_reconstruct does not
exist" — WITHOUT ever emitting the TOOL line — and parked the tower and
phase nodes at B/N on that false premise. The verbs were there; it just
declined to try them and narrated absence instead.

New guard: verbs_falsely_called_absent scans the draft for any line that
pairs an unavailability phrase ("not available", "does not exist", …)
with the token of a verb that ACTUALLY exists (STRUCTURAL_VERBS or the IG
corpus), token-boundary matched so "set" doesn't fire inside "onset".
When the agentic loop is about to accept a final answer that denies a
real verb, a bounded PHANTOM-VERB PROD (≤2) pushes back — "that is false,
`ascend` is live, emit its TOOL line now" — and forces the call before
the answer stands. If the verb genuinely returns B, that B is now earned,
not asserted.

Two tests pin it (catches the real denial; ignores substrings and
verb-less unavailability). Suite green (29 passed).
