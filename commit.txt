Anchor the natures to their kernel constructors; nothing is invented

I had been hedging that grounding a nature's Belnap imscription would
need a value-to-code rule I mustn't invent. That was wrong: the whole
thing stands on a Lean kernel, so the rule is a kernel fact, not a guess.
p4rakernel Core.lean defines the twelve families as inductives, and the
49 primitive types ARE their 49 constructors: KineticChar is
yea < loll < egg < on < air, so egg is that inductive's ordinal-2
constructor, and 3^3 * 4^5 * 5^4 is the crystal. The value-to-Belnap
character is delivered by the kernel (Paradice.lean δ/μ with μ∘δ = id,
OrbitalBelnap.orbToB4, TupleCodec's tuple↔address roundtrip), and the
paradice is a kernel object: depth × Frobenius-verified windings, not the
Hamming count I sketched in Python.

So natures.py now binds each Nature to its real kernel family, vessel
key, and constructor ordinal, taken from Core.lean rather than guessed by
gloss. KERNEL_FAMILIES carries the twelve inductives with their ordered
constructors; the three TYPES-directory spellings that differ from the
Lean ones (if→if', or→or', ha-ha→haha) are normalized in
kernel_constructor(). Registry.tiles_crystal() proves the 49 types are
exactly the 49 constructors, each once, and by_kernel_family() returns an
axis in kernel order. Self-test asserts the tiling and that egg is
KineticChar ordinal 2.

The correction to the record: the vessel imscription of a known type is
grounded by evaluating through the kernel (the prover bridge), not by an
LLM and not by a fabricated table. The natures now hold the kernel
coordinate needed to do that. Exposed KERNEL_FAMILIES and
kernel_constructor at package level.
