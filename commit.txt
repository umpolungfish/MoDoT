Extract every TOOL: directive, including multiples per line and zero-arg

The tool-call parser was line-anchored and required at least one arg, so
two directives on one line collapsed — the second was swallowed as the
first's args and silently dropped (seen in the transcript: crystal_count
absorbed the following excite call, 19 of 20 emitted calls ran). Zero-arg
verbs like crystal_count only matched by accident when they absorbed the
next directive.

extract_tool_calls now splits on the literal TOOL: marker anywhere it
appears: each call runs to end-of-line or the next TOOL:, whichever comes
first, and zero-arg verbs are captured cleanly. The marker is case-
sensitive (the model emits TOOL: uppercase for a real directive), so prose
"tool:" does not false-trigger. Two tests pin it: the exact one-line
double-directive from the transcript, and lowercase-prose immunity. Suite
green (31 passed).
