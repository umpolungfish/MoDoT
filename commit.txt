Add the crystallization bench: crystallize, cocrystallize, seed

The second alchemical family, ordering-to-a-solid, grounded on the ob3ect
typings (alchemical_tools_roadmap) and built on the existing ring/charge
machinery rather than new metrics:
  - crystallize M1 M2...   grow the ordered lattice from a pool via
    best_ordering: the units that enchain are lattice-bound (EVALT), the
    rest are rejected into the mother-pool (EVALF). A closed ring is a
    crystal (T), a partial fit is the interfacial layer (B), nothing
    enchaining is amorphous (F). Distinct from anneal, which only relaxes
    an already-closed loop; this forms one from a set and rejects the
    supernatant, exactly as the oracle's stereochemical-filter typing.
  - cocrystallize A B       one NON-covalent lattice of two components held
    by best_weak_complement (opposite charge on a live pair) in 1:1
    stoichiometry, no reaction center consumed. If they also click
    covalently, that bond is the stronger option and the cocrystal is the
    non-bonded co-lattice; no complement is charge frustration (B).
    Distinct from click (covalent fusion).
  - seed M1 M2 ... with S   template-directed: units whose handedness Ħ
    (index 9) matches the seed copy its polymorph (EVALT, templated), the
    rest take the default (EVALF, spontaneous), an even split is racemic
    twinning (B). Non-conservative, the arms fuse to total crystalline
    mass, the one op the oracle typed with a fuse_result different from the
    input.

Wired through the Cli flags and the agent verb whitelist (run_structural_tool
+ verb_usage + STRUCTURAL_VERBS + TOOLS_PROMPT + no-op-prod cues), same as
the volatility family. Verified live on real catalog entries across the T,
B, and F paths (a closed crystal, a frustrated cocrystal and a formed one,
a fully templated seed).
