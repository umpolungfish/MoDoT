Report open problems as frontiers, not "beyond the Grammar's reach"

Answers to open problems kept coming back with defeatist prose like
"formal proof remains outside the current reach of the Grammar's
established closure." My prover path (prover.rs) already has the right
discipline everywhere — non-closure is a navigation frontier B, never
"impossible", never "unprovable", never a limit of the Grammar — but the
manuscript/witness path never carried that stance, so the model filled the
gap on its own with the barrier/out-of-reach framing.

I traced the injection to three prompt strings and reworded all three to
the frontier register, in plain mathematical language (the system prompt
also bans kernel cosplay, so no Belnap jargon leaks to the model):

  - SYSTEM_PROMPT (main.rs): "open, with barrier ..." becomes "for a
    genuine open problem, precisely what is proved, what remains open, and
    the concrete obstruction / next line of attack — never call a result
    unprovable or beyond the reach or closure of the Grammar; an open
    problem is a frontier to push, not a wall";
  - the manuscript instantiation task (main.rs): "prove or give a rigorous
    barrier" becomes "prove it, or if open, state precisely what remains
    open and the next line of attack — never 'unprovable', never beyond
    the Grammar's reach";
  - build_conventional_scaffold (witness_proof.py): "what the grammar
    predicts as the barrier" / "open, barrier = ..." become "what is
    proved, what remains open, and the concrete next line of attack ...
    an open problem is a frontier to push".

Rebuilds clean. Prompt-only change: it steers what the model is told, so
in-flight jams keep their old wording and the next run picks up the fix.
