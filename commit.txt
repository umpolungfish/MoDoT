Add --jam: turn the agent loose on the catalog, no question

The toolset is built out enough that the right move is to stop steering it. Jam
mode hands the agent the full toolset and a seed (a lemma, a pile of context,
--file, or nothing) with NO question to answer and NO objective to climb -- it
forges, fuses, cleaves, dopes, anneals, compares, clicks, follows its own
curiosity, and combines tools in ways nobody asked for.

The design rests on one principle, the golem rule scaled to free exploration:
constrain the OUTPUT, free the PROCESS. The whole reason it is safe to unleash
the process completely is that honesty is already enforced at the output boundary
-- the executed-verb binding means the final write-up can state only what a tool
actually returned. So there is no interestingness gradient, no curated signal to
hunt, no leash on where it wanders. Freedom in the doing, truth in the report.

Reuses the entire ManuscriptSpine agentic loop; jam only swaps in JAM_PROMPT,
reframes the seed as a starting point rather than a question, raises the round
budget to 40 so it can actually range, and forces the act-prod so it jams rather
than narrating. Everything that keeps it honest (executed-verb binding, cap
guard, golem synthesis) is untouched.

  ./ask --jam "some lemma or pile of context"
  ./ask --jam --file context.md
