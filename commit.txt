Rebuild --expand as a degree-of-detail dial with a structural-identity gate, not an LLM line-count target

I first built --expand N as "make the proof roughly N lines." That is the wrong
object twice over. It let the model chase a line count and drift, and longer
renderings never even reached the kernel: a Mathlib STYLE linter
(linter.style.whitespace, "commands should start at the beginning of the line")
was erroring on the model's indentation, and my compile gate treats any "error:"
as not-green. A style linter is not the kernel. It was silently acting as a
second gate in front of it, vetoing proofs that were kernel-valid.

I routed the design decision through an ob3ect instead of settling it by my own
preference. My first entity smuggled the verdict in ("deterministic elaboration
ladder" versus "an LLM targeting a line count", "forcing the choice between"), so
the oracle handed my bias straight back, twice. Re-imscribed neutrally, saying
only what I want the thing to be (a component that renders the conventional proof
at a caller-chosen degree of detail, every rendering closing the identical kernel
theorem with no loss of structural fidelity), the structure told me something I
had not put in: the dial is not a choice of generator at all. It is a reversible
expansion and contraction morphism over ONE kernel theorem, the invariant is
structural identity at the fusion, and the pinched form and the walked-out form
are held together in multi-scale superposition. The "LLM versus deterministic"
fork was mine, never the object's.

So this does three things:

- Disables the style-linter family for the scratch build (disable_style_linters,
  STYLE_LINTER_OFF): every registered Bool-valued linter.style.* option switched
  off, inserted right after the imports where top-level set_options are legal. A
  kernel-valid rendering can no longer be blocked on indentation, line length, or
  bullet form. The kernel (unknown identifier, type mismatch, sorry) stays the
  sole discriminator. admit still cannot slip through: it elaborates to sorry,
  which the build output reports and the compile gate already rejects.

- Adds the fidelity gate (renders_same_theorem), the structural-identity invariant
  the neutral ob3ect requires: a rendering at any detail level is accepted only if
  it closes the identical statement, igFrobeniusAlg.mul s0 s0 = s0 with the same
  tuple in all three positions. This makes any generator safe rather than banning
  the model. Template or LLM, the statement must be byte-identical to the pinched
  anchor, so a fuller rendering can never quietly prove a weaker proposition.

- Reframes --expand from a line count to a degree of detail: 0 is the pinched
  minimal form, greater than 0 is the walked-out form via the expansion morphism.
  The detail directive and the token budget were reframed to match, dropping the
  "walk toward N lines" framing entirely.

Verified end to end: the mis-indented but kernel-valid theorem that used to error
on the whitespace linter now builds green; LUCA at detail 2 renders the walked-out
validity proof (unfold tensorProduct, Imscription.ext, one simp per primitive
coordinate) over the real lucaImscription, the vessel intact and the statement held
identical, closed green on the first pass; LUCA at detail 0 still closes the
pinched igFrobAlg_self_fusion form. Scratch modules restored to placeholder after
each run.
