I gave the loop learned relations and gave programs promotion paths.

Two changes, one insight connecting them.

Relations feedback: the loop was recording (object, word) pairs but feeding
only the glyph-slip confusions back to the imscriber. Now, before imscribing an
object, it retrieves the nearest confirmed relations (prior objects that were
excribed from known words, ranked by word-overlap, ties to the lower residual)
and shows them as examples. This is the learning channel the loop was missing:
the imscriber generalizes the object-to-word direction from confirmed cases,
and the recovered words go from the old 2-4 glyph collapse to full-length words
of the right family. Legacy empty-guess rows still in the file are inert here
(zero overlap, never retrieved).

Promotion paths: two tuples that differ have a promotion path, one primitive
changed at a time with every intermediate well-formed. Two IMASM programs are
no different. `imasm path A B` finds the shortest walk of single-opcode edits
from A to B in which EVERY waypoint is a valid program: A* over valid programs
with the raw edit distance as the admissible heuristic, either face. The learn
loop's residual is that raw distance; the promotion path is the stronger object
on the same metric, and its verdict can climb along the way. Substituting an
inert IMSCRIB for a working opcode promotes an identity closure N to a real
closure T in one step, the program-space image of a tuple promotion. Guide gains
Part XV.

Verified: build green under local,cuda; 7/7 learn tests (two new for the path:
single-substitution length and all-waypoints-valid); live paths shown lateral
(T to T), climbing (N to T), multi-step with insertions, and across the tri face.
