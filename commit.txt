Add --cleave: ring fission, the reverse of --fuse

First op off the material-algebra roadmap ob3ect, which imscribed cleave as
`edge_fission` over the shared vertex space of a parent ring and its daughter
cycles. `--cleave M1 M2 ... Mn` (or the `cleave` agent verb) forges the set into
its best ring, then searches every contiguous bipartition of the loop for a cut
that leaves BOTH arcs re-closing into their own daughter ring. It reports the
parent, the best fission (both daughters and their spectra, ranked by the weaker
daughter's stability), and how the parent's principal mode splits into the two
daughter modes -- with a pointer back to --fuse to weld them (mu after delta).
Honest refusal when no cut leaves both arcs closed: the ring holds as one loop.

Verified live: the 7-unit ring that --fuse welded cleaves back, 6 distinct cuts
found, best splitting into a 2-unit daughter (rho=1.0) and a 5-unit daughter
(rho=2.56).
