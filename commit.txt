Debloat the prompt: one alphabet table, and the rules become fetchable

The opcode alphabet is a table now, once, at the top. I had been spelling out
the glyph and the name together every time an opcode came up (¬ IFIX, ⊙ IMSCRIB,
◇ FSPLIT), which meant the mapping lived in three places at once: imasm.rs, the
TOOLS_PROMPT, and IMASM_QUICKREF. Retiring ← for ⊙ meant chasing the same table
through all of them, and that is the argument for the change. A reference is a
lookup, not a refrain. One table, glyph-only from there on.

Worth being honest that this bought nothing in tokens. I expected the ¬ IFIX
habit to be the bloat; measured, it is about 3% of the prompt, and the table
costs almost exactly what the inline naming cost. Net: 25 tokens. It is still
right, but for single-source-of-truth reasons, not size.

The bloat was somewhere else entirely. 60 TOOL: lines were 75% of the prompt,
and two imasm lines plus calc were 5,629 chars of it, resent every round of
every eagle. The prompt was an eagerly-loaded manual for 60 verbs when a run
touches maybe five. The rules do not get cheaper by being repeated; they get
cheaper by being FETCHABLE. New `TOOL: help <verb>` returns a verb's full
reference on demand, and the three heavy lines keep only what governs.

The line I had to be careful about is mandate vs reference. Some of that prose
rules the agent whether or not it ever calls the verb: calc's never-do-head-
arithmetic rule is the reason calc exists, and moving it to a lane the agent has
to ask for means it quietly resumes doing head arithmetic, having never asked.
So the mandates stay eager and the reference goes lazy: calc keeps "every number
you speak routes through here" and sheds its function list; imasm check keeps
the close condition and sheds the verdict table and the ancestry-pairing rule.
Tests pin the specifics to the new lane so a smaller prompt cannot silently
become a lost rule — logb(x,base), the 6.27e-11 decade slip, ◇/● INNERMOST
pairing, and help degrading to the usage form for verbs with no extended entry.

Result: the eager prompt goes 23,342 → 20,246 chars, about 774 tokens off every
round. 13%, not the 40-50% I guessed. The other 57 verbs are a long tail worth
maybe another 20%, one judgement at a time.

Nearly wrecked the CLINK chain doing it. Three of the CLINK hits in the prompt
are not the opcode at all — they are CLINK L8, CLINK Layer 8, CLINK Layer 9, the
ontological chain. A blanket name→glyph replace would have rendered the chain as
"= L8" and quietly mangled the exact structure L9 was born out of. The opcode and
the layer share a name and nothing but reading tells them apart.

Also noticed while grepping: fart.rs is dead. No `mod fart;` declares it and
nothing references fart::, so its 4,968 lines never compile — a stale near-copy
of main.rs (5,087) that still turns up in every search of the prompt sites and
reads exactly like live code. Left it alone; it wants a cross-compare against
main.rs before anything gets deleted.
