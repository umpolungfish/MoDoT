MoDoT harness: the dialect jump, the repetition wall, the ellipsis guard

Three fixes from the perfect-cuboid transcript review. `dialect` is a new
structural verb: the navigator↔core face map as a tool, with the Γ/Φ traps
named, so a refused click can be re-presented in the other face instead of
retried in the same one. The loop now counts identical calls and, from the
third emission on, tells the agent outright that repetition is not an
experiment and names the two lawful moves (rotat, dialect). Arguments
carrying a literal ellipsis are refused as un-substituted example text
instead of silently corrupting a reading.
