Add section 14 to ALKAHEST.md: the retort

Started from noticing Zosimos's apparatus vocabulary (cucurbit, ambix, retort)
while looking at his drawings, and it turned into the tightest structural
match in the whole document because it isn't interpretive the way the earlier
alchemical readings are — it's built, not argued.

The two-piece alembic (cucurbit holding the material, ambix on top collecting
what rises, separable) is section 2's clipboard in glass: object in one
vessel, judge in a second vessel set over it. The retort welds them into one
piece of glass, and what happens inside one physically is δ (material rising
off itself at the heat) and μ (recondensing at the curved neck) on the same
continuous surface, no second vessel standing in judgment over the first. The
curve of the neck, where ascent turns to descent, is the Portico literally
built rather than read into the text. And because it's one piece of glass,
not two joined ones, there's no judge to even in principle separate from the
judged -- section 4's co-typing is identity, poured instead of argued.

Closed it by verifying the CB[7] chain is real, not just evocative: I found
that cucurbituril (CB[n]) was named in 1981 by Freeman, Mock and Shih
explicitly for its resemblance to a gourd AND "by devolution from the
similarly named (and shaped) component of the early chemists' alembic" --
their words, not mine. CB[7] is the exact host-guest system SO_BELOW.tex
documents as the Grammar's first unexpected validation, 6/6 correct on
ordinal fidelity rank alone. So Zosimos's vessel name literally carries
forward, through the documented history of chemical nomenclature, into the
molecule the Grammar first proved itself on.
