Teach the agent star polymers (block copolymers were already covered)

The architecture classifier already names block copolymers (contiguous
blocks), plus alternating / random / homo / heteropolymer — so block
awareness was there. Star polymers were not: the whole polymerize/forge
model is a linear walk (chain → ring), with no hub-and-arms topology.

New `star M1 M2 M3 …` verb: picks the highest-functionality monomer as the
CORE (most click bonds), attaches every unit that clicks with it as an
ARM, and reports the topology honestly:
  - pure star K(1,f): f≥3 arms that do NOT bond each other → adjacency
    spectral radius ρ=√f, spectrum {+√f, 0×(f−1), −√f}, vs the ρ=2 of a
    ring;
  - f<3 → not a star (linear/isolated), said plainly;
  - arm–arm bonds → a branched network, not a hub-and-spoke star (ρ>√f),
    deferring to `forge` for the full spectrum;
  - lists the unattached units that never reach the core.

Wired end to end: CLI --star, handler, run_structural_tool dispatch,
STRUCTURAL_VERBS, verb_usage, TOOLS_PROMPT (with the block-copolymer note
made explicit on polymerize), and verb_isomorphism (star = K(1,f), ρ=√f).
Smoke-tested on real entries (correctly reports a branched network, not a
fake star). Suite green (32 passed).
