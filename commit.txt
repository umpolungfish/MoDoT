Drop the arbitrary round ceiling: run until done or stalled, dedupe calls

I was capping the agent's tool rounds at 5 (40 in a jam) and forcing a
"step limit" close mid-investigation. A round budget is a wall, and a wall
is the same defeatism I just removed from the prose register. Now --eagles N
is an explicit leash when I ask for one; without it the loop runs until the
operator stops emitting tools (done) or stalls: three consecutive rounds of
nothing but calls that already ran and returned. A 400-round backstop guards
only the pathological infinite loop.

Live telemetry also showed the operator emitting exact duplicates in one
batch (anneal A B twice, click X Y twice, plus re-emitting a call that ran
that same round), each burning a slot under the 6-per-round cap. Calls are
now deduped within the round before the cap applies. The per-round cap
itself stays: it is pacing (act, then read, then act), not a budget.

The forced close now fires whenever the loop ends with calls still pending
(leash, backstop, or stall) and says why, instead of only on the old step
limit. Builds clean, all 21 tests pass.
