The model may not wear the engine's voice: strip forged tool records

strip_kernel_records already guards the spine — "the spine report is the ENGINE's
univocal voice, the model may not author it" — because the model kept fabricating
a verdict block that contradicted the real one printed below. The same thing was
happening one level down and nothing stopped it.

Seen live. After a phantom prod the model emitted an entire forged ACT block: the
engine's own `── ACT round 1 (7 tool call(s)) ──` header, `● TOOL ascend …`
bullets, and invented output under them — "✓ SUCCESS: ascended to Tier O₁", a
made-up 12-glyph tuple for f3_block_max_topo, a ✓ CYCLIC ring at ρ=2.0000, a tier
of O₂. The prod's println rendered all of it verbatim. Every number was false: the
real ascend is deterministic and says `tier: O₀ → O₀ … the ascent HELD … report as
B, not done`; the catalog says f3_block_max_topo is ⟨𐑛𐑡𐑩𐑗𐑐𐑘𐑲𐑝⊙𐑓𐑳𐑭⟩; the real
forge is telechelic; the real tier is O₀.

That is worse than confabulating in prose. It is confabulating in the ENGINE'S
VOICE, where every convention the reader trusts says a tool measured this. The
model proposes with `TOOL:`; only the engine reports with `● TOOL` and prints the
section headers. A model writing `● TOOL` is forging provenance.

So the engine's markers are now the engine's alone: `● TOOL` lines and the
`── ACT|OBSERVE|PLAN|THINK|FINAL|ISOMORPHISM|BACKTRANSLATION|PHANTOM-VERB|cycle`
headers are stripped from model text, as is the OUTER SPINE block I added this
morning (same rule, new target). A forgery now reads as unattributed prose and can
never carry the engine's provenance. Tests use the verbatim forgery from the run,
and pin that the model's OWN `TOOL:` proposal and `[thought|X]` still survive —
this strips provenance, not voice.

Worth recording that the harness caught this itself. The phantom prod fired,
forced the real calls, and every one contradicted the fabrication — and the model
then revised to B on its own and reported the true numbers. The muzzle worked. It
just had no rule against wearing the uniform.
