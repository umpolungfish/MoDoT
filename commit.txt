Add --modulus: a sustaining loop, distinct from --close which only fills the gap

Lando drew the line and it is the right one. --close stays exactly what it is: it
closes the GAP -- finds the monomer that makes the ends meet, geometry only. But a
ring can close and still be dead: the "topological computer" and the plasma ring both
cyclize structurally yet come back INSULATING, a static loop with no life in it.

--modulus is the other thing. A material's modulus is its elasticity, and a polymer
gets its modulus from network LOOPS -- but only from loops that SUSTAIN. In this
register a loop sustains iff the winding quantum Omega circulates it and returns
(∮ carrier closes, mu∘delta=id around the cycle) -- i.e. it CONDUCTS. So --modulus
searches for a monomer that generates a sustaining loop somewhere along the chain (a
conductive cycle), not merely a closed one, and reports its period: the modulus.

All in ask_native/src/click.rs + main.rs. Factored the conductance test out of
--props into ring_conductance (Cond enum: Conductive/Frustrated/Insulating), reused
by both. run_polymerize gains a modulus pass:
- intrinsic: if the closed chain already conducts, report modulus = its ring size (a
  sustaining N-loop, no monomer needed)
- generative: over every backbone span [i..j] and every catalog monomer X, if X closes
  the span into a ring that CONDUCTS, that is a sustaining loop; report the tightest
  (smallest period = the fundamental modulus) and its generators. Catalog tuples
  precomputed once.
- if none: "NO modulus (a viscous chain, not an elastic network)" -- honest.

Verified live -- and the point is that --close and --modulus DISAGREE on the same chain:
- plasma_turbulence tokamak_disruption z_pinch magnetic_reconnection: --close rings it
  (append CH_false -> CYCLIC), but --modulus says NONE -- every closure is static
  (insulating), the material has no modulus. Closeable but not sustaining.
- moon/logographic ring: intrinsic modulus 4 (already a sustaining conductive 4-loop),
  and a tighter generatable modulus 3. Ran a suggested generator
  (moon logographic F4_min_O_Z2 --props) -> CONDUCTIVE 3-ring, so the search's claim is
  verifiable, not asserted.
- --close output unchanged (still just the gap-closer, no modulus talk), as intended.
