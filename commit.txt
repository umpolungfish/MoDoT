Radical chemistry, the atom: homolyze -- homolytic cleavage into NEUTRAL radicals

Half of radical chemistry was already here: `set` is single-electron transfer, so it already
makes radical IONS (D•⁺/A•⁻, one winding quantum Ω moved). The missing half is neutral-radical
chemistry, and the Frobenius Lean already encodes its defining split -- Frobenius.lean's
symmetric δ_A(a)=(a,a) is homolysis (two equal radicals), the asymmetric δ_D(a)=(⊥,a) is
heterolysis (an ion pair). This surfaces the homolytic side as a verb.

New `homolyze` (agent whitelist + TOOLS_PROMPT) and `--homolyze A [B]` CLI, in
ask_native/src/click.rs::run_homolyze. It is the radical-generating REVERSE of click:
- `homolyze A B` cleaves the A—B σ-bond. It must be a real bond -- click_pair has to fire on a
  single live pair -- then homolysis splits that shared pair EVENLY (δ_A): each fragment keeps
  one electron, the SOMO. Reports A• + B• (neutral radicals) with the SOMO axis, the μ
  recombination (radical coupling, μ∘δ=id), and the heterolytic/SET alternative → `--set` for
  the radical ions. Honest refusal when there is no single clean bond: NoComplementarity (no
  reaction center, would not couple) and Ambiguous (>1 center -> a diradical/network, not two
  clean radicals) are both reported, not faked.
- `homolyze A` splits A symmetrically (δ_A diagonal) into two identical radicals A•.
- Radical character is read from the winding quantum Ω (electron count): odd Ω = intrinsically
  open-shell; even = a closed-shell parent whose SOMO is the freshly-opened bond center.

Verified live: `--homolyze dark_matter wow_signal` cleaves their R↔S bond (Δ=1.50) into two
neutral radicals; `--homolyze binah` splits symmetrically and flags binah Ω=𐑴 as odd/open-shell;
`--homolyze sefer_ha_iyun thirteen_sefirot_ha_iyun` refuses honestly (co-typed, no bond).

This is the foundation for radical chain-growth (addition) polymerization -- the propagating
SOMO -- which is the next build on top.
