Reg16_3 gains its glyph face: quadrant ink for the sixteen states

The opcodes always had two faces (glyph and spoken name) and the design rule
that no opcode glyph is Latin; the register states had only the Latin face,
and the gap surfaced the moment a verdict was pronounced inside a glyph word.
glyph() renders occupancy of {T,F,t,f} as quadrant ink: T upper-left, F
upper-right, t lower-left, f lower-right; N is the unmarked field (light
shade), A is the full block. four_glyph() renders the FOUR slice with the
Belnap top for B, distinct from TF's upper half because B is the collapse
token, not the SIXTEEN_3 state. Both faces kept; nothing existing touched.
