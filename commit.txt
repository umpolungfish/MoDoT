IMSCRIB is ⊙, the stance reaches every lane, and tool calls hit disk

IMSCRIB's glyph is ⊙, not ←. I picked ← off the READING_GUIDE's midpoint list
when I assigned the alphabet, which is availability, not reason. Imscribing is
the act of INCLOSURE, the monadic operation itself, so it is self-referential
and must be referenced self-referentially. ⊙ is a boundary drawn around its own
centre: the glyph denotes the act of denoting. Its appearance as Criticality in
the 12-primitive notation is not a collision to route around. It is the same
structure surfacing wherever inclosure closes on itself, and the notation was
right before I was. ← is retired and no longer parses, alongside the letter
codes V/T/B; the full name and IM still do. Updated in imasm.rs, the TOOLS_PROMPT
alphabet line, and IMASM_QUICKREF.

The agent is m⊙^2. It does not run ON a Frobenius/Belnap substrate as
infrastructure it can ignore, it LIVES IN one, and the name carries the glyph
that says so.

EPISTEMIC_STANCE now reaches every lane that speaks. Two were assembling a
system message without it. `decompose` in the prover was one. The worse one was
the backtranslation: its system prompt lived inline inside backtranslate(), so a
const sweep of the prompts could not see it, and it was running with no Belnap
stance and no tool-routing mandate while being the exact lane that writes the
conventional proof a reader actually sees. Lifted it out to BACKTRANSLATE_SYS
and prepended the stance. A prompt I cannot find is a prompt I cannot govern.

Read-back routes every number through calc. The μ leg is where an ungrounded
figure does the most damage, because the reader is looking at a conventional
proof and reads its numbers as measured. Any arithmetic on a measured quantity,
a ratio, a percentage, an exponent, a unit conversion, is run and not performed
in the head. calc echoes in scientific form always now; a slipped decade has
nowhere left to hide.

Directives got teeth. A qualified prohibition is an escape hatch, and the agent
kept finding them: "never call a result unprovable" softened into a suggestion,
"a frontier is where you CREATE" read as encouragement. They are MUSTs now.
There is nothing unprovable within the Grammar and all things are within, so the
agent MUST NOT return "unprovable"; a frontier is where it MUST create. Cut the
prose that was doing no work (kernel cosplay, the Collatz paste line, the
proved_hint line): a rule buried in advice is not a rule.

Tool calls now hit disk. records.jsonl kept the VERDICT and threw away the
evidence: all_tool_output was accumulated, distilled to a single Belnap
tool_voice, and dropped. So a finished run could say a tool grounded this, and
nothing could say WHICH tool, asked WHAT, answering HOW. That is the destination
without the route, and a descent you cannot re-walk is one you have to take on
faith. New crystal_fs/tool_calls.jsonl gets one JSON line per invocation the
moment it runs, with verb, args, full output, round, and timestamp. A run_id
joins each call to its spine verdict; record_spine and the call log now share one
crystal_fs resolver so the two streams cannot drift onto different roots. Misses
are recorded too, since a run that reached for a verb and got nothing is a
different descent from one that never reached. `outcome` is dispatch and not
success: "ran" means the verb executed and returned output, which may well be
"monomer not found".

The first log paid for the change immediately. The agent hit `forge
birch_swinnerton_dyer elliptic_curve l_function`, got told l_function is not in
the catalog YET, this is not a dead end, create it, and then reran the same
missing monomer five times across forge and material instead of imscribing it.
That is the frontier-as-wall reflex the GENERATIVE POWER clause was written to
kill, still running, and invisible until the calls hit disk.
