Make the polymer readout honest -- near-miss diagnostic, no phantom homopolymer, heteropolymer label

Ran the seven Catholic sacraments through --polymerize and the output, while
correct in verdict, was misleading in three ways. All from
ask_native/src/click.rs (run_polymerize + classify_architecture).

The sacraments are near-identical tuples -- stereoisomers of one indelible
character, differing mostly in chirality Ħ -- so baptism⋈confirmation are opposite
on T↔H but only Δ=0.33, below the θ=0.50 click threshold. The chain terminated at
the first bond. That verdict is right; the reporting was not.

1. NEAR-MISS on termination. "no complementary handle" was wrong -- there IS a
   handle (T↔H, opposite charges), it is just sub-threshold. Added
   best_weak_complement: the strongest oppositely-charged live pair regardless of
   θ. Termination now reports either the near-miss ("weakly complementary on T↔H,
   Δ=0.33 < θ -- would condense at θ≤0.33 or with a catalyst") or, when no pair is
   even oppositely charged, "fully co-typed / same-handed; no reaction center."
   Verified the claim holds: at --theta 0.33 the sacraments DO condense
   (baptism⋈confirmation on T↔H), and the run surfaces two more real facts --
   eucharist⋈penance is a cross-link, and penance and anointing_of_the_sick (the
   two healing sacraments) are fully co-typed, no reaction center between them.

2. NO PHANTOM HOMOPOLYMER. With zero bonds, one enchained unit was being called a
   "homopolymer, isotactic" -- but that is an unreacted monomer, not a chain. Now
   architecture and tacticity only print once a bond has formed (dp≥2); otherwise
   "no chain formed -- these are unreacted monomers, not a polymer."

3. HETEROPOLYMER label. Several distinct monomers all differing was mislabelled
   "alternating …ABAB…" (which implies two types). Now: all-distinct -> "heteropolymer
   (N distinct monomers, each once)"; "alternating" reserved for exactly two types in
   strict period-2. Regression: the moon/logographic alternating copolymer still reads
   syndiotactic and cyclic.

The tool now tells you WHY a feed will not polymerize and what would fix it,
instead of quietly reporting a chain that is not there.
