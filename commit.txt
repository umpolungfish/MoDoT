Add --fuse: weld two ring materials into one

The last material-algebra operation I left out. `--fuse A B C + X Y Z` (or the
`fuse` agent verb) forges each ring, then forges the union of their units into a
single macrocycle, and reports how the fused ρ and conductance relate to the two
parents — whether the weld adds branching (fused ρ above both parents) or is a
clean splice (fused ρ within the parents' range). A union that will not re-close
is reported honestly: the two rings do not fuse into one material.

Reuses material_of + the `+` separator token, mirroring --compare (`vs`) and
--dope (`with`). Verified live: fusing a pure 4-ring (ρ=2.0) with a branched
3-ring (ρ=2.732) welds into a 7-unit macrocycle at ρ=2.517, read as a clean
splice.
