Close the catalytic cycle -- the loop as a certified fixed point (Solve et Coagula)

Every verb so far was an ARC of a mechanism -- bind (complement), transform
(set/excite), hold (scan-mediators). This closes them into the LOOP, and it turns
out to be Solve et Coagula made literal.

--cycle CATALYST SUBSTRATE (run_cycle in ask_native/src/click.rs):
- bind (δ / CLINK): the catalyst recognizes its substrate via the ligand
  complement (tuple_dist to complement_type(catalyst)); reports the grip.
- working stroke (δ / SOLVE): dissolves the bond, frees one winding quantum Ω --
  the degree of freedom. Reuses transfer_electron both directions (reductive =
  catalyst donates, oxidative = catalyst abstracts); substrate -> product, the
  catalyst spent to C*.
- return stroke (μ / COAGULA): binds the freed quantum into an invariant; the
  catalyst coagulates back to itself (terminal redox restores the resting state).
- turnover: net substrate -> product, catalyst returned unchanged -- a FIXED
  POINT of the loop. TWO coagulations: the catalyst binds back to the SAME
  invariant (regeneration, μ∘δ=id); the substrate binds into a NEW invariant
  (the product). Solve freed one quantum; Coagula placed it.

--certify proves the catalyst is a fixed point: certify_switch(C, C*) shows both
states are valid Imscriptions AND readback(board p)=p, i.e. Coagula∘Solve = id on
the catalyst (μ∘δ=id, lossless regeneration), through lake build. --register
canonizes the turned-over product and decomposes it through the cl8nk_navigator.
Honest guard: a pair where neither redox direction is feasible (both Ω pinned)
gets "no turnover", not a fake cycle.
