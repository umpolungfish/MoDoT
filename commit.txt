Retry transient LLM read-timeouts instead of poisoning a grounded cycle

A long synthesis (the cycle-12 SIC report) can outrun the socket read
deadline: the body is arriving but slowly, and into_json() returns
"timed out reading response". That is transient transport, not a
malformed reply, but the code collapsed it straight to voice:'F' with a
parse-error text. Downstream that F trips the "narrated tools, ran none"
prod and the ENGAGR fusion reports UNGROUNDED, holding an otherwise fully
tool-grounded cycle at N. The tools had run; only the final read hiccuped.

infer() now wraps the provider call in a short retry (two attempts, 0.5s
and 1.0s backoff) gated on is_transient_llm_error, which fires on read/
connect timeouts and dropped connections but NOT on a genuine malformed
body or an API error payload (retrying those just resends a bad request,
so they keep their F). Two unit tests pin both directions; full suite
green (26 passed).
