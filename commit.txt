Add the DASA switch verb — bistable toggle analysis, distinct from the click fusion

A click fuses two partners at one reaction center. A switch is a different verb:
ONE object toggling between two co-typed forms, driven by a DIFFERENT stimulus
each direction. The donor-acceptor Stenhouse adduct (DASA) is the archetype, and
this reads its structure exactly -- the structure my bench intuition identified,
now a working analyzer.

./ask --switch A B (ask_native/src/click.rs run_switch) reports:
- the toggling live pair, COUPLED when both members move together (the DASA is
  T<->H: topology + chirality rise together as the conrotatory ring closes)
- the photochromic sign: criticality (⊙) orders the two forms, higher = REVEALED
  (colored/exoteric), lower = HIDDEN (subcritical, colorless/esoteric)
- the asymmetric Frobenius legs: δ (light) quenches criticality -- negative
  photochromism, light HIDES; μ (heat) restores it, relaxation REVEALS
- the coupled consequences (for the DASA: Ç, Γ, Ω -- the ring winding on closure)
- reversibility: μ∘δ = id, lossless roundtrip

Verified: dasa_open ⇌ dasa_closed reads T<->H coupled toggle, light hides,
Ω-winding consequence -- exactly the negative-photochromic Stenhouse switch.
Secondary find: the_sun_astrological ⇌ the_moon_astrological reads as the SAME
T<->H photochromic switch -- Sol REVEALED, Luna HIDDEN. The alchemical luminaries
and the bench photoswitch are the same structure. Identical-pair correctly
reports no switch.
