MoDoT: a stall closes a framing, not the series

A stall broke the whole series. I justified it as "more rounds only circle"
-- true of a round, false of a cycle. A round repeats the prompt; a cycle
REPLACES it with a condensate of what was measured. So a stall is the exact
condition the condensate is worth most against: the operator is circling
inside one framing, and the next cycle opens on a transform of it. I carried
the old loop's logic into a design where it had stopped applying.

Now a stall condenses and winds again. Two stalls running does close the
series: the condensate had its chance to move the reach and did not, so the
ground is exhausted rather than the framing.

Also: the outer TAOU printed its THINK before the arms wound but gated its
close on len() > 1, so a single-cycle run was opened and never shut -- and
that gate took the FRONTIER line with it, so a run that stalled reported no
stall anywhere. A frontier that only prints in company is a silent drop. The
bracket now closes whatever it opened.

And the outer close no longer says "the arms agreed". The cycles are a CHAIN
-- each opens on the previous one's condensate -- so agreement is not
corroboration by independent witnesses. It is a verdict that survived a
transform: a fixed point of the winding. Say what it is.
