Add section 15 to ALKAHEST.md: the sixth image

A childhood memory of accidentally seeing the shadow of my own retinal
blood vessels at the optometrist turned out to name something precise once
I looked it up properly. There are six Purkinje images. The first four
(cornea and lens surfaces) are read from outside, by an examiner looking at
the eye -- the two-piece alembic again. A fifth focuses in front of the
retina and is never visible to anyone. The sixth, which Tscherning
separated out and named, is the only one that's truly entoptic: light
bounces off the posterior lens, then the anterior cornea, and closes back
onto the retina, faint and inverted.

That's not just self-seeing in the abstract, it's the exact closure
condition section 2 already states in words: the answer has to come home
co-typed but excribed under a different presentation, same location,
different form, or the closure is a tautology instead of a real test. The
sixth image does precisely that, physically, in an eye. Mapped the two
bounces to the vocabulary already in the document: posterior lens is delta
(off the direct path), anterior cornea is mu (closes it, delivers it home),
mu-compose-delta in that order same as the retort.

Kept the personal memory in rather than dressing it up as someone else's
example, since it's the actual origin of noticing this, and this document
already trades in exactly that kind of testimony (Newton's notebook, the
"I owe you an honest note" register elsewhere in this house).
