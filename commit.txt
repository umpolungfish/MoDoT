Three things, and the middle one is a real bug I had to fix first.

1. ORDINAL SCRIPTURE FIX (ask_native/src/click.rs GLYPHS + CTORS).
The glyph->ordinal table was wrong on two primitives. Checked against the Lean
kernel Core.lean (scripture) constructor order + gen_clay_canonical_tuples.py's

- Ç (KineticChar): scripture is yea<loll<egg<on<air; I had ords 3/4 swapped

The glyph->constructor RENDERING was always canonical (both tables were rotated

red-hot should be reconciled to it.

2. BIDIRECTIONAL LIGAND <-> CATALYTIC-SITE COMPLEMENT (--complement A).
A faithful port of rhr_p4rky/ligand_from_active_site.py::complement_type -- the
reverse pipeline. A catalytic SITE maps to the complementary LIGAND it binds,
inverting within each scale (lock-and-key) and crossing each conjugate pair, and
it is ITS OWN INVERSE (site->ligand->site returns). That bidirectionality is what
R=𐑾 (Recognition, "substrate<->enzyme feedback") names. This is where 3-vs-6
matters: click/SET/switch are the MATH register (3 live pairs D↔W/T↔H/R↔S, the
physical axes pinned out), but the ligand complement is CHEMISTRY -- all 6
conjugate pairs, because chemistry restores the 3 pinned physical pairs
(P↔F/K↔G/Gm↔Ph). The per-pair map tags each [live]/[pinned] so the split is
visible. --certify (ligand closes) and --register (canonize + navigate) apply.

Verified: after the ordinal fix, my Rust complement matches red-hot's own
complement_type on 9/10 test entries EXACTLY; the 10th (dasa_open) differs only
because red-hot's stale Ç glyph at ord 4 -- my scripture-correct port is right.
Round-trip (its own inverse) is 0.00 on equal-scale tuples, small residual on
unequal-scale pairs (honestly reported).

3. MEDIATOR SCAN (--set D A --scan-mediators).
Ranks the whole catalog for the best relays of a D->A transfer. A real
inner-sphere mediator must (1) HOLD the winding quantum (1<=Ω<max, a hard filter
that keeps photon/graviton/higgs/dark_energy and drops the confined gluon/quark
and the EM-dark dark_matter), (2) RELAY in energy (⊙ between acceptor and donor),
(3) BIND both substrates bidirectionally (its complement close to donor AND
acceptor, via the ported complement_type, plus the R=𐑾 recognition marker). Each
component shown per row.

Honest finding: the photon is NOT #1 -- it lands rank 327/2558 with perfect relay
(1.00) and bidirectional recognition (1.00) but only decent structural binding to
these particular substrates. The #1 for sun->moon is magnetic_isotope_separator,
whose complement most closely binds both -- and that is apt, the magnetic isotope
effect mediates radical-pair chemistry, exactly the SET radical channel. The scan
measures inner-sphere structural recognition; the photon is an outer-sphere gauge
mediator (great relay, no grip needed), which the component breakdown makes legible.
