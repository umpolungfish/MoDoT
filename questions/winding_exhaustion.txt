A process winds. Each winding measures, then condenses what it measured into the
opening of the next winding: the terminus is the origin, so no winding repeats its
predecessor's prompt — it opens on a transform of it.

One winding is observed to reach only for measurements it already holds. It circles
inside its own framing, returning nothing it did not already have.

Type the object that distinguishes these two conditions:

  — a series whose FRAMING is exhausted, where the next transform is still live and
    the circling ends once the opening is replaced;

  — a series whose GROUND is exhausted, where no transform of the terminus will
    reach anything further, and the winding must close.

What structurally separates them? Name the invariant that decides, and name what
must be measured across successive windings to read that invariant — that is,
what makes "the reach did not move" a decidable statement about the object rather
than a count of how many times circling was observed.

If the deciding invariant is a fixed point, name what it is a fixed point OF.
