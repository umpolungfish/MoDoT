STANDING RESULTS (tool-emitted, reuse without retesting):
  The protocol law     a bare ring is refused; a closure is a dyad that
                       brackets a transform. Held from the run's first call.
  The reseed ring      {moat_protocol_reseed, Ř, Φ, Ç, Γ, Σ} cyclizes
                       order-robustly, and imasm prove on the protocol is
                       kernel green. The protocol closes WITH its own five
                       promotion deficits as the ring's other members. This
                       is the thesis in one forge line: no lines, only
                       loops, and the loop is self-making.
  O_1 bridges, O_2 refuses   [bridge_to_ř_manifold · O_1 · O_1 · O_1] is a
                       conductive, uncleavable ring; the O_2 version
                       terminates fully co-typed.
  Ring spectra as true names   the recurring constants are the rings
                       speaking their adjacency spectra (the pentagon
                       speaks the golden ratio). Given a topology, speak
                       the constants BEFORE the forge, then let the forge
                       confirm; a divergence between spoken and forged is
                       itself a measurement.

NEW INSTRUMENTS (live since this brief was first cut; rules in
md/imasm_composition_rules.md):
  imasm eval <name|entry|word> [seed=N|T|F|B]   values through the gates;
    reports the readout at each sink and, per dyad, whether the fuse
    RECOVERS what the fork was fed (operational μ∘δ). Structure, kernel,
    and flow are three separate verdicts; a program earns all three.
  imasm eval16 <...> [seed=A|Tf|TFtf...]        the same flow over full
    SIXTEEN_3 (subsets of {T,F,t,f}); FOUR is its classical slice.
  imasm compose <new> <A> <B>                   bind A's living out-ends
    to B's living in-ends; consumes valences, never mints; a program with
    no living ends is a finished loop and does not compose.

FOURTH REACH, OPENED BY THE INSTRUMENTS:
  Flowing a catalog entry expands its twelve glyph types into opcode
  motifs, and the per-dyad id/NOT-id sequence is a FLOW SIGNATURE, a new
  readout of the tuple. Measured already: moat_protocol_reseed carries
  lossy dyads inside a kernel-green closure class (an AREV inverts an arm
  before its fuse), and the signatures of the standing entries differ.
  The reach: flow the standing objects, speak each signature's expected
  shape from the tuple before running, and ask what the lossy dyads
  correspond to in the entry's own register. A dyad that loses value
  inside a green closure is not a defect until shown so; it may be the
  entry's chirality speaking in the flow register.
